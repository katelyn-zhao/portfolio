Machine Learning Differentiation of Liver Disease via Fat Distribution MRIs | Python

  • Investigated the utility of MRI-Proton Density Fat Fraction (PDFF) as a non-invasive biomarker for differentiating NAFLD and NASH, reducing the reliance on invasive liver biopsy

  • Identified Couinaud liver regions 1, 2, 3, and 8 as critical for enhancing diagnostic accuracy, with PDFF Interquartile Range (IQR) values outperforming median values in ROC-AUC and classification metrics

Liver Segmentation in Medical Imaging | Python

  • Implemented multiple advanced segmentation models, specifically convolutional neural networks, to predict and segment the liver from 3D MRIs and CT scans for further analysis of liver characteristics and future diagnosis of liver disease
  • Compared model performance across multiple different architectures, including U-Net 2D and 3D, U-Net++, Seg-Net, V-Net, and ResU-Net++, achieving dice scores of up to 0.96
  • Integrated those models into the first AI driven software for fatty liver disease diagnosis named LiverSeg-i, creating a non-invasive MRI tool for diagnosing liver disease
